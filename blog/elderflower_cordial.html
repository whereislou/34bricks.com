Elderflower Cordial

This recipe comes from Diana Henry's Salt Sugar Smoke (one of the most beautiful and inspiring books for gardeners with an excess of produce) and it is a keeper.

Ingredients

  • Around 25-30 heads of fresh elderflower
  • 3 non-waxed lemons
  • 75g citric acid (which you can get at the supermarket in the baking section)
  • 1.5 litres water
  • 1.5 kg white granulated sugar

Bring the sugar and water to the boil in a saucepan. Stir to help the sugar dissolve. Zest the lemons and then slice them, dividing the zest and lemons slices evenly among two large 2 litre jars (or a large 4 litre container). Pour over the boiled sugar water, add the citric acid and stir. Pop the lids on and leave the jars somewhere cool for about 24 hours.

The next day strain the cordial through a sieve lined with muslin. Pour into sterilised bottles and keep in the fridge for up to 5 weeks.

I love using elderflower cordial in drinks (e.g. a lovely refreshing gin & tonic or soda), but it can also be used in a dressing or a sauce (e.g. in a salad dressing with a bit of olive oil and lemon juice). It also makes a delightful gift!
